# Design Calibration

This redesign intentionally avoids AI product-launch and dark SaaS references. The calibration set below was used for direction only: palette, typography, navigation cues, and spatial rhythm. It is not a copying list.

## Transit / Wayfinding

- [Transport for London Maps](https://tfl.gov.uk/maps/)
  - Clear sectional navigation, strong route labeling, and wayfinding-first information hierarchy.
- [MTA Subway Map](https://new.mta.info/sites/default/files/pdf/subway_map_app_web_Jan_2019_unlock.pdf)
  - Dense but legible map language, practical labeling, and utility-led visual contrast.

## Editorial Travel / Hospitality

- [Kinfolk](https://www.kinfolk.com/)
  - Warm editorial spacing, restrained typography, and calm reading rhythm.
- [Ace Hotel New York](https://acehotel.com/new-york/)
  - Hospitality-forward art direction, tactile surfaces, and less app-like page composition.

## Museum / Cultural

- [MoMA Ticketing](https://go.moma.org/ticketing)
  - Visit-planning framing, direct calls to action, and strong emphasis on pathing through an experience.
- [Guggenheim Plan Your Visit](https://www.guggenheim.org/plan-your-visit)
  - Cultural-institution tone, visitor-first organization, and itinerary-friendly presentation.

## Translation To This Site

- Move to a light, paper-based palette with darker ink text and warmer accents.
- Treat navigation like wayfinding: section markers, route context, and clear trip facts first.
- Prefer folio, roster, route, and board metaphors over dashboard, console, or command-center metaphors.
- Keep planner density high where it helps decision-making, especially on the attractions page.
